About Theorem

A mistake-first tutor for Algebra 1.

Theorem is a mistake-first tutor. It helps students understand why an answer went wrong instead of just giving the answer.

Theorem’s core loop is simple: try a problem, get a diagnosis, repair the exact skill, then prove mastery with a fresh problem. The interface is calm on purpose because students learn better when feedback feels clear instead of punishing.

01

Diagnose the break.

Wrong answers are classified into mistake patterns like sign errors, skipped operations, or distribution mistakes.

02

Repair quickly.

Each repair is shorter than a lesson and focuses on the missing move.

03

Prove mastery.

A fresh problem confirms the student learned the idea, not just the answer.
